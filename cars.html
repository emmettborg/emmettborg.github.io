Car Shopping on Kijiji

I am slightly addicted to online car shopping on Kijiji. It's fun to imagine myself owning a certain car, or hunting down a (seemingly) great deal.

Kijiji is a free classifieds website where private sellers and dealers can post vehicles for sale. Some details are included in these ads such as the year, make, model, mileage, and price. Other useful information may also be in the free-text description. Most shoppers likely have a specific vehicle or budget in mind, and can filter through the thousands of ads. Looking through a long list of ads may be daunting for some shoppers though. Especially when looking for something like a Honda Civic or Toyota Corolla.

How does one know they found a good deal or not? Is it worth shopping at dealer or can you save money by going private? In general, I felt that Kijiji didn't provide insight into the overall marketplace.

1. Web Scraping

I built a web-scraping program in python to pull search results from a Kijiji. Essentially, it scrolls through each ad at a time while collecting key attributes. Now I have a table/database to work with. Keeping record of ads is also great for tracking price changes, and seeing which ads are taken down (assumed to have sold).

If you are shopping for a newer vehicle however, the only option may be to buy from a dealer as the number of private listings dwindle.

2. Finding a Deal

Key Considerations

  • "All that glitters is not gold". A low price may be due to things like previous accidents, body damage, or required maintenance.
  • Vehicles may come in different "trims". A 2013 Honda Civic for example came in a DX, LX, EX, and Si which retailed for $15k, 18k, 20k, and 26k respectively.
  • Location may play a factor. Supply and demand in Toronto (pop: 3M) is much different than in Sudbury (<200K).
